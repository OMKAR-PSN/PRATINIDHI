NATIONAL SCHOLARSHIP PORTAL (NSP) & EDUCATIONAL WELFARE - KNOWLEDGE BASE 2026

1. NATIONAL SCHOLARSHIP PORTAL (NSP) - MISSION AND ARCHITECTURE
The National Scholarship Portal (NSP) is a digital mission mode project under NeGP.
It provides a common electronic portal for implementing various scholarship schemes.
The portal is hosted at the official URL: scholarships.gov.in.
It aims to provide a simplified, mission-oriented, and transparent system for students.
NSP ensures "Direct Benefit Transfer" (DBT) of funds into the students' bank accounts.
It eliminates leakage and duplication of applications through Aadhaar integration.
The portal supports schemes from Central Ministries, State Governments, and UTs.
As of 2026, NSP has implemented a "One-Time Registration" (OTR) system.
OTR provides a unique 14-digit permanent ID to students for their entire academic life.
Aadhaar Face Authentication is now mandatory for OTR for all students above 5 years.

2. SCHOLARSHIP CATEGORIES AND SCHEMES ON NSP
2.1 Pre-Matric Scholarship Schemes:
- Target Group: Students studying from Class 1 to Class 10.
- Ministries: Ministry of Minority Affairs (MoMA), Social Justice (MoSJE), Tribal Affairs.
- General Eligibility: Family income must not exceed Rs. 2.5 Lakh per annum.
- Scholarship Amount: Ranges from Rs. 3,500 to Rs. 7,000 per annum.
- Focus: Reducing the drop-out rate in primary and secondary education levels.
- SC/ST/OBC and Minority communities are primary beneficiaries.

2.2 Post-Matric Scholarship Schemes:
- Target Group: Students in Class 11, Class 12, ITI, Diploma, and Higher Education.
- Includes Graduate, Post-Graduate, and Ph.D. level students.
- Income Limit (SC/ST): Rs. 2.5 Lakh per annum.
- Income Limit (OBC/EBC): Rs. 1 Lakh to Rs. 1.5 Lakh depending on the state.
- Benefits: Covers compulsory non-refundable fees and maintenance allowance.
- Maintenance allowance for hostellers is higher than for day scholars.

2.3 Merit-cum-Means (MCM) Scholarship:
- Specifically for professional and technical courses (Engineering, Medical, Law).
- Target: Minority communities and economically weaker sections.
- Eligibility: 50% marks in the previous final examination.
- Annual Income Limit: Rs. 2.5 Lakh.
- Benefit: Full tuition fee reimbursement (capped) and maintenance allowance.

2.4 PM-YASASVI (Young Achievers Scholarship Award Scheme for Vibrant India):
- For OBC, EBC, and DNT students studying in Top Class Schools and Colleges.
- Selection: Merit-based through the YASASVI Entrance Test or direct NSP application.
- Scholarship: Up to Rs. 75,000 per year for Class 9-10; Rs. 1,25,000 for Class 11-12.

3. STEP-BY-STEP NSP APPLICATION PROCESS (2026 UPDATE)
Step 1: Obtain OTR (One-Time Registration) via the NSP mobile app or website.
Step 2: Complete Aadhaar Face Authentication or OTP-based verification.
Step 3: Log in using the OTR ID and the password sent to the registered mobile number.
Step 4: Fill the Scholarship Application Form with academic and domicile details.
Step 5: Select the specific scheme for which the student is eligible.
Step 6: Upload Mandatory Documents:
- Educational Marksheets (Previous Year).
- Domicile Certificate issued by the competent state authority.
- Income Certificate issued by Revenue Officer/Tahsildar.
- Caste Certificate (SC/ST/OBC/Minority Status).
- Bank Passbook (Aadhaar-seeded account is mandatory).
- Bonafide Student Certificate from the Head of the Institute.
Step 7: Final Submit and take a printout for institutional verification.
Step 8: L1 Verification: Conducted by the Institute Nodal Officer (INO).
Step 9: L2 Verification: Conducted by the District/State Nodal Officer (DNO/SNO).
Step 10: Merit List Generation and Payment processing via PFMS (Public Financial Management System).

4. PM POSHAN SHAKTI NIRMAN (MID-DAY MEAL SCHEME)
PM Poshan is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme to improve the nutritional status of children.
It covers all school children in Classes 1-8 in Government and Government-aided schools.
It also covers children in Special Training Centres and Madrasas supported under Samagra Shiksha.
The scheme has been extended to "Balvatika" (children below Class 1) as of 2024-25.

4.1 Nutritional Standards (Per Child Per Day):
Primary (Classes 1-5):
- Calories: 450 kcal.
- Protein: 12 grams.
- Food Grains: 100 grams (Rice/Wheat).
Upper Primary (Classes 6-8):
- Calories: 700 kcal.
- Protein: 20 grams.
- Food Grains: 150 grams (Rice/Wheat).
Iron, Folic Acid, and Vitamin A supplementation is provided in coordination with NHM.

4.2 Key Features of PM Poshan 2.0:
- Tithi Bhojan: Encouraging the community to provide special food on festivals/birthdays.
- School Nutrition Gardens (SNG): Schools grow their own vegetables and fruits.
- Social Audit: Mandatory audit in every district to ensure food quality and hygiene.
- Cooking Cost: Central and State governments share the cost of pulses, oil, and fuel.
- Honorarium: Cook-cum-helpers receive a monthly honorarium for their services.
- Millets (Shree Anna): 2026 update mandates inclusion of millets at least once a week.
- Tamil Nadu Special: Provides free breakfast for Classes 1-5 in all government schools.

5. RIGHT TO EDUCATION (RTE) ACT, 2009
The RTE Act makes education a Fundamental Right under Article 21-A of the Constitution.
It provides free and compulsory education to all children aged 6 to 14 years.

5.1 Section 12(1)(c) - Reservation in Private Schools:
- Mandates 25% reservation in private unaided schools for EWS and disadvantaged groups.
- Disadvantaged groups include SC, ST, Orphans, HIV-affected, and Disabled children.
- The government reimburses the tuition fees to the private schools at per-child costs.
- Admission is usually conducted through a centralized state-level lottery system.

5.2 Key Provisions and Mandates:
- No Detention Policy: No child can be held back or expelled until Class 8.
- No Screening: Schools cannot conduct entrance tests for children or interviews for parents.
- No Capitation Fee: Charging extra fees for admission is strictly prohibited and punishable.
- Pupil-Teacher Ratio (PTR): 30:1 for Primary; 35:1 for Upper Primary.
- Infrastructure: Mandates separate toilets for girls/boys, drinking water, and playgrounds.
- Working Days: 200 days for Primary; 220 days for Upper Primary per academic year.

6. PM SHRI SCHOOLS (SCHOOLS FOR RISING INDIA)
PM SHRI is a centrally sponsored scheme to develop 14,500 "Exemplary Schools."
These schools act as mentors to other schools in their respective clusters.
They showcase the implementation of the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

6.1 Core Pillars of PM SHRI Schools:
- Pedagogy: Experiential, holistic, integrated, and learner-centered education.
- Assessment: Competency-based assessment focusing on conceptual clarity.
- Green Schools: Solar panels, LED lights, waste management, and water harvesting.
- Infrastructure: STEM labs, smart classrooms, libraries, and vocational labs.
- Modern Facilities: High-speed internet and digital pedagogy tools.
- Selection: Schools apply through an online portal based on 60 defined parameters.
- Funding: Direct funds are provided to schools to meet specific local learning needs.

7. OTHER CRITICAL EDUCATIONAL SCHEMES
7.1 National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS):
- For Class 8 students to prevent drop-outs before secondary school.
- Exam: State-level Mental Ability Test (MAT) and Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT).
- Benefit: Rs. 12,000 per year from Class 9 to Class 12.

7.2 INSPIRE Scholarship:
- For top 1% students in Board Exams pursuing Basic and Natural Sciences.
- Benefit: Rs. 80,000 per annum for B.Sc. and M.Sc. courses.

7.3 PM-Vidyalaxmi:
- A unified portal for students seeking Education Loans from various banks.
- Links scholarship portals with loan portals for seamless financial planning.
