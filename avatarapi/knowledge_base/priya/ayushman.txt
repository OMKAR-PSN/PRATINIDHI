AYUSHMAN BHARAT - PRADHAN MANTRI JAN AROGYA YOJANA (PM-JAY) KNOWLEDGE BASE 2026

1. MISSION SCOPE AND CORE OBJECTIVES
Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY is the world’s largest government-funded health assurance scheme.
It was launched to achieve the vision of Universal Health Coverage (UHC).
The scheme is designed to provide financial protection against catastrophic health expenses.
It provides a health cover of Rs. 5 Lakh per family per year.
The cover is applicable for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization.
PM-JAY is a completely cashless and paperless service at the point of care.
The scheme covers over 12 crore poor and vulnerable families (approx. 55 crore individuals).
As of 2026, it is integrated with the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM).

2. DETAILED ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA (Socio-Economic Categories)
Eligibility is primarily based on the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 data.
Rural Categories Eligible:
- D1: Only one room with kucha walls and kucha roof.
- D2: No adult member between ages 16 to 59.
- D3: Female-headed households with no adult male member between 16 to 59.
- D4: Households with a disabled member and no able-bodied adult member.
- D5: SC/ST households.
- D7: Landless households deriving a major part of their income from manual casual labor.
Urban Categories Eligible (Based on Occupational Hazards):
- Beggars and those living on alms.
- Domestic workers and Ragpickers.
- Street vendors, cobblers, and hawkers.
- Construction workers, plumbers, masons, and painters.
- Sweepers, sanitation workers, and mali (gardeners).
- Transport workers (drivers, conductors, helpers), cart pullers, and rickshaw pullers.
- Shop workers, peons in small establishments, and delivery assistants.
- Waiters, washermen, and mechanics.

3. SPECIAL EXPANSION: SENIOR CITIZENS (70+ YEARS)
In late 2024, the Union Cabinet expanded PM-JAY to all citizens aged 70 and above.
This expansion is income-agnostic (available to rich and poor alike).
Senior citizens (70+) belonging to families already covered under PM-JAY get an additional 
top-up cover of Rs. 5 Lakh per year (shared only with other 70+ members in the family).
Senior citizens (70+) not covered under PM-JAY get a standalone cover of Rs. 5 Lakh per year.
Seniors already covered under private insurance or ESIC are also eligible.
Seniors covered under CGHS or Ex-Servicemen Contributory Health Scheme (ECHS) can choose 
between their existing scheme or PM-JAY.

4. HEALTH BENEFIT PACKAGES (HBP 2026)
The scheme covers 1,961 medical procedures across 27 specialties.
Specialties include:
- Cardiology and Cardio-thoracic Surgery (e.g., Pacemaker, PTCA, CABG).
- Oncology (Medical, Surgical, and Radiation therapy for all types of cancer).
- Nephrology (e.g., Dialysis, Kidney transplant assistance).
- Orthopaedics (e.g., Knee/Hip replacement, complex fracture management).
- Neurosurgery (e.g., Brain tumor excision, spinal surgeries).
- Paediatric Surgery and Neonatal Care.
- Ophthalmology (Cataract surgery, Vitreo-retinal surgery).
Pre-hospitalization: Covers diagnostics and medicines for 3 days prior to admission.
Post-hospitalization: Covers medicines and consultation for 15 days after discharge.
Room charges, nursing, food, and OT/ICU charges are included in the package rates.

5. HOSPITAL EMPANELMENT AND PORTABILITY
Total empanelled hospitals exceed 36,000 across India.
The network includes both Public (Government) and Private providers.
National Portability: A beneficiary from one state can receive treatment in any other state.
Hospitals must meet Quality Council of India (QCI) standards for empanelment.
Tier-1, Tier-2, and Tier-3 cities are covered to ensure rural-to-urban access.

6. THE AYUSHMAN CARD AND DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE
The Ayushman Card (e-card) is the primary identity document for the scheme.
Cards are generated using Aadhaar-based e-KYC (Face-Auth or OTP).
The Ayushman App allows for self-registration and card downloads.
ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account): A 14-digit number to link digital health records.
ABHA allows beneficiaries to share lab reports and prescriptions with doctors digitally.
The BIS (Beneficiary Identification System) manages the verification of eligible members.
TMS (Transaction Management System) handles the processing of hospital claims.

7. EXCLUSION CRITERIA (For standard categories, not 70+):
- Households owning a motorized 2/3/4-wheeler or a fishing boat.
- Households with a Kisan Credit Card (KCC) limit above Rs. 50,000.
- Households with any member as a government employee.
- Households earning more than Rs. 10,000 per month (as per original SECC).
- Households paying Income Tax or Professional Tax.
- Households owning 2.5 acres of irrigated land with irrigation equipment.

8. GOVERNANCE AND GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
National Health Authority (NHA) is the apex body for implementation.
State Health Agencies (SHA) manage operations at the state level.
Ayushman Mitra: Dedicated help-desk personnel at every empanelled hospital.
National Helpline: 14555 or 1800-111-565.
Grievance Portal: CGRMS (Central Grievance Redressal Management System).

9. MAHILA SAMMAN BACHAT PATRA (MSBP)
Type: One-time small savings scheme for women and girls.
Tenure: 2 years (Fixed).
Interest Rate: 7.5% per annum (Compounded quarterly).
Minimum Deposit: Rs. 1,000.
Maximum Deposit: Rs. 2,00,000 per account holder.
Withdrawal: 40% of the balance can be withdrawn after 1 year.
Account Closure: Can be closed prematurely in case of death or extreme medical grounds.
Accessibility: Available at all Post Offices and scheduled commercial banks.

10. MAHARASHTRA MAJHI LADKI BAHIN YOJANA
Purpose: To provide financial independence and health nutrition to women in Maharashtra.
Financial Benefit: Rs. 1,500 per month (Direct Benefit Transfer).
Age Limit: 21 to 65 years.
Income Limit: Combined family income must be less than Rs. 2.5 Lakh per annum.
Mandatory Documents:
- Aadhaar Card.
- Maharashtra Domicile Certificate or Ration Card (Orange/Yellow).
- Bank Passbook (Aadhaar seeded).
- Self-declaration form.
Exclusions: Families with a permanent government employee or an Income Tax payer.
Application Portal: Nari Shakti Doot App or local Setu Suvidha Kendra.

11. SWACHH BARET MISSION (SBM)
Objective: Achieving Open Defecation Free (ODF) Plus status for all villages and cities.
SBM-Gramin: Focus on rural sanitation.
SBM-Urban: Focus on garbage-free cities and scientific waste processing.
Individual Household Latrine (IHHL) Incentive: Rs. 12,000 for BPL/Identified APL families.
ODF Plus Categories:
- ODF Plus Aspiring: Villages with functional toilets and basic waste management.
- ODF Plus Rising: Villages with advanced liquid/solid waste management.
- ODF Plus Model: Villages with complete sustainability and visual cleanliness.
Gobar-Dhan Scheme: Converting cattle dung and organic waste into Biogas and Bio-CNG.

12. PM KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI
Benefit: Rs. 6,000 per year provided in three equal installments of Rs. 2,000.
Installment Periods: April-July, August-November, and December-March.
Eligibility: All landholding farmer families (subject to exclusion rules).
Exclusions: Institutional landholders, former/present constitutional post holders, 
retired employees with a pension of Rs. 10,000 or more, and professionals (Doctors, Engineers).
Mandatory: e-KYC and Aadhaar seeding are required for receiving payments.
Portal: pmkisan.gov.in for self-registration and beneficiary status tracking.

13. PM AWAS YOJANA (PMAY) - SUMMARY DATA
PMAY-Gramin: Rs. 1.2 Lakh (Plains) / Rs. 1.3 Lakh (Hilly) for house construction.
PMAY-Urban 2.0: 1 crore houses targeted for urban poor and middle class.
Interest Subsidy: 4% interest subvention for home loans up to Rs. 8 Lakh for EWS/LIG.
Convergence: Includes SBM (Toilet), Saubhagya (Electricity), and Ujjwala (LPG).
