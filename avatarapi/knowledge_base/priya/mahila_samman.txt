MAHILA SAMMAN AND WOMEN EMPOWERMENT SCHEMES - KNOWLEDGE BASE (2026 EDITION)

1. MAHILA SAMMAN SAVINGS CERTIFICATE (MSSC) - CENTRAL GOVERNMENT
The Mahila Samman Savings Certificate is a one-time small savings scheme for women.
It was launched by the Ministry of Finance to promote financial inclusion for women.
The scheme is available for a two-year period, currently extended through March 2026.
It offers a fixed interest rate of 7.5% per annum, which is higher than most bank FDs.
The interest is compounded quarterly and credited to the account.
The tenure of the investment is exactly two years from the date of deposit.

1.1 Investment and Withdrawal Rules:
- Minimum Investment: Rs. 1,000 (and in multiples of Rs. 100).
- Maximum Investment: Rs. 2,00,000 across all MSSC accounts held by an individual.
- Multiple Accounts: A woman can open multiple accounts with a 3-month gap between each.
- Partial Withdrawal: Allowed up to 40% of the eligible balance after 1 year.
- Premature Closure (General): Allowed after 6 months with a 2% interest penalty (5.5% rate).
- Premature Closure (Special): Allowed without penalty on the death of the holder or life-threatening disease.

1.2 Eligibility and Process:
- Any woman or girl child (represented by a guardian) who is a resident of India.
- No upper age limit for women applicants.
- Where to open: Any Post Office or authorized Public/Private sector banks (SBI, ICICI, HDFC, etc.).
- Documents: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, and recent photographs are mandatory.
- At maturity, the full principal plus compounded interest is paid via a linked bank account.

2. MUKHYAMANTRI MAJHI LADKI BAHIN YOJANA (MAHARASHTRA)
This is a flagship social security scheme of the Maharashtra Government for women.
The primary objective is to provide economic independence and improve health/nutrition.

2.1 Financial Benefits:
- Monthly Assistance: Rs. 1,500 per month.
- Annual Total: Rs. 18,000 per year provided via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).
- The amount is credited around the 15th of every month.

2.2 Eligibility Criteria:
- Residence: Must be a permanent resident (domicile) of Maharashtra.
- Age Limit: Women aged between 21 and 65 years.
- Income Limit: Combined family income must be less than Rs. 2.5 Lakh per annum.
- Marital Status: Married, widowed, divorced, abandoned, and destitute women are eligible.
- Special Category: One unmarried woman per family is also eligible.

2.3 Exclusions and Conditions:
- Families where a member is an Income Tax payer are excluded.
- Families with a regular government employee or pensioner are excluded.
- Families owning a four-wheeler (excluding tractors) are not eligible.
- Mandatory: The bank account must be Aadhaar-linked and have e-KYC completed.

3. MUKHYAMANTRI MAHILA SAMMAN YOJANA (DELHI)
A welfare initiative by the Delhi Government to provide direct cash support to adult women.
The scheme aims to bridge the gender gap in financial resources.

3.1 Benefit and Eligibility:
- Monthly Benefit: Rs. 1,000 for every eligible woman in Delhi.
- Age: Must be 18 years or older.
- Residency: Must be a registered voter and resident of Delhi.
- Requirement: Must NOT be receiving any other government pension.
- Requirement: Must NOT be a government employee (State, Central, or PSU).
- Required Documents: Aadhaar card, Voter ID of Delhi, and a functional bank account.

---

4. MAHILA SAMRIDDHI YOJANA (MICRO-FINANCE)
This is a micro-credit scheme for women belonging to Backward Classes (SC/ST/OBC).
It is implemented through State Channelizing Agencies (SCAs).

4.1 Loan and Repayment:
- Maximum Loan Amount: Up to Rs. 1,40,000 per beneficiary.
- Interest Rate: Lower interest rates for women entrepreneurs (usually 4% to 6%).
- Repayment Period: Up to 3.5 years, including a moratorium period of 3 months.
- Usage: For starting small businesses, tailoring, beauty parlors, or retail shops.

4.2 Eligibility for MSY:
- Women from SC/ST/OBC categories as notified by the government.
- Family income must be below Rs. 3 Lakh per annum.
- Age: Between 18 and 55 years.

5. LAKHPATI DIDI AND DRONE DIDI SCHEMES (SHG EMPOWERMENT)
These are 2026-priority schemes focusing on rural women in Self-Help Groups (SHGs).

5.1 Lakhpati Didi Initiative:
- Target: To empower 3 crore rural women to earn at least Rs. 1 Lakh per year.
- Training: Technical skills, digital banking, financial literacy, and entrepreneurship.
- Support: Market linkages and access to credit for scaling up small businesses.

5.2 Drone Didi Scheme:
- Training: Women from SHGs are trained as drone pilots and technicians.
- Objective: To modernize agriculture by using drones for spraying fertilizers/pesticides.
- Benefit: Women earn a livelihood by providing drone services to farmers.
- Equipment: The government provides drones and training grants to selected SHGs.

6. ADDITIONAL WOMEN-CENTRIC SCHEMES
6.1 Stand-Up India for Women:
- Loans between Rs. 10 Lakh and Rs. 1 Crore for setting up greenfield enterprises.
- At least one woman borrower per bank branch is mandatory.
- Interest rate is the lowest applicable rate of the bank for that category.

6.2 TREAD Scheme (Trade Related Entrepreneurship Assistance):
- Provides a 30% government grant for projects involving women entrepreneurs.
- The remaining 70% is provided as a loan by lending institutions.
- Focuses on developing entrepreneurial skills and providing market information.

6.3 Mahila E-Haat:
- An online marketing platform to support women entrepreneurs and SHGs.
- Women can showcase their hand-made products directly to buyers without middlemen.
- Registration is free and does not require any commission from sales.

7. APPLICATION SUMMARY AND DOCUMENTATION
7.1 General Document Set:
- Aadhaar Card (Primary proof of identity).
- Domicile Certificate (To prove state-specific residency).
- Income Certificate (Issued by the Tahsildar for the current financial year).
- Bank Passbook (Clearly showing IFSC and Aadhaar-seeding status).
- Ration Card (Yellow/Orange for income verification).
- Self-Declaration Form (Affirming that no other pension is being received).

7.2 Digital Access:
- Most state schemes are accessible via the "Nari Shakti Doot" or "Majhi Ladki Bahin" apps.
- Central schemes are monitored via the "MyScheme" portal.
- All payments are made through the Aadhaar Payment Bridge (APB) to ensure transparency.
